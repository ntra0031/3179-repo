Alcohol: its Consumption and Effects Globally

There is no question that alcohol has been around for a long time and with it comes the discussion of what a healthy consumption is. Every country and culture around the world will have their own views on drinking and while it is more common for some countries such as Italy or Russia for people to start drinking when they are younger, it is more frowned upon in others such as Yemen and Pakistan.

This chloropleth map shows the estimated average consumption of alcohol by adults (aged 15+) for one year. The country with the highest consumption rate is Moldova at 23.01 litres followed by Russia and several countried in the Eastern European areas. There are some countries where data is not available such as Congo and the United States of America which are marked as grey areas.
